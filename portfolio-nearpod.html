Integration of Nearpod into Star

With the acquisition of the popular instructional platform Nearpod, Renaissance took a big step toward realizing the grand vision of a seamless journey linking assessment, instruction and practice. Of course, the interesting part is taking that theoretical value and making it a reality. The first piece of that work was to integrate Nearpod lessons into our flagship assessment product, Star, allowing us to: 1) Make instructional recommendations based on what we know students and instructional groups are ready to learn. 2) Call attention to areas of need for students working in the core curriculum and provide extra supports as appropriate.

Screenshot

As always, with every opportunity comes a number of challenges, and this work was no exception. First, it's always risky to add new features, especially in a product as mature as Star. No matter how important a feature is, it's also a frustrating disruption to existing workflows.

For another, fundamental differences in how the two existing platforms function present some major challenges around interoperability. Most notably, without shared rostering, and without a robust concept of student identity in Nearpod, it is impossible to track student progress across the two platforms. So, given that we are limited to providing a set of deep links from Star to Nearpod, how might we ensure that the right students are getting the right instructional recommendations?

Screenshot

Speaking of existing workflows, in a product as full as Star there's a range of legacy features. One opportunity we identified early on is that we can unify multiple competing workflows around instructional recommendations into one. In order to manage change responsibly, this unification process is broken into phases, giving users lead time to try the new experience and offer feedback. Once we feel confident that our customers have voluntarily migrated to the new experience, we will begin to deprecate what has become obsolete. We set up reporting to track usage of each of these workflows in order to ensure our decisions were driven by data.

Differentiated Instruction

Typically, the focus of this type of instruction is on individual students or small groups. In this use case, the system must recommend a set of skills that these students are ready to learn, and provide resources and guidance to help teach these skills. The teacher needs assessment data to guide the placement of students into small groups, with the ability to override those recommendations. From there, the system identifies appropriate Nearpod lessons for each group.

Screenshot

Core Instruction

This type of instruction is mandated by an official curriculum. The teacher is beholden to a predetermined schedule, and the whole class works together. We can make it easy for the teacher to find the skills they are scheduled to teach via both search and browse navigation models. In addition to providing resources for the skills the teacher is accountable for teaching, we can also provide insights into which students may struggle with these skills, and in those cases provide a catch-up plan.

Screenshot

In the spirit of letting the user work at the level of their expertise, throughout this process we put the most important and actionable information at the surface level, and more in-depth, detailed views a layer deeper.

None of this is successful if it's not easy to understand and use. Through these types of decisions, validated with user testing, we are confident that we are on the right path, and also fully aware that we will hear from our customers where we can improve.
