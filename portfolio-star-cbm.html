Star CBM

Star CBM was released in September, 2020, after a year of research, design and development. Targeted at early elementary students and teachers, it rounds out an exciting suite of established and successful assessment products. As our first assessment to be administered directly by the teacher to the student, with each test scored in real time over the course of one minute, we knew the potential for frustration and anxiety in the testing experience was high. Furthermore, as we planned to make the assessment available online and in print, we needed to make sure that the teacher-student browser interactions felt intuitive and performant. To that end, we did extensive user testing integrating the real coded scoring experience into an Axure prototype of the full teacher experience.

The project got underway with a cross-functional design workshop, mapping the teacher journey for pain points and opportunities. We aligned on a high-level vision and reimagined the user quotes we hoped to hear.

Design decisions throughout the project were driven by a focus on the experience. We saw a great opportunity in creating a space for teachers and students to interact one-on-one, rather than in whole class or small group instruction. The teacher and student journeys are optimized for quick and easy assessment scoring, and positive feedback to the student for effort. Based on user feedback, we were successful in helping facilitate meaningful, quality time between teachers and students.

We also wanted to make sure that, from log-in to completion, the teacher experience of administering an assessment is logical and frictionless, and provides immediate value. We honed that workflow through extensive iterative usability testing, and have gotten excellent feedback, both quantitative and anecdotal, about the product's ease of use compared to its competitors.

Another opportunity we identified was to integrate data and recommendations into the core journey. Star CBM is the first Renaissance Learning product to explicitly recommend student interventions, and to take the teacher through the process with smart defaults and best practices built in. Class- and student-level data is also incorporated directly into the application, rather than in a separate reporting portal, so it is visible and available at key decision making points.

We knew from talking to teachers and observing how they collect and act upon assessment data, that there was a tremendous opportunity to pull results from multiple assessments into one view to help paint a complete picture of student achievement. Doing so involved making changes to the core Star assessments workflow, which was a risk, as it represented a major change to a high-profile and high-usage area of the platform. In this case, the change was very well-received. The Star Record Book, arguably the most important screen in the Renaissance platform, is now receiving higher satisfaction scores than before.
