Star CBM — Data Insights

We know that dashboards are among the most over-requested and under-used features in data-centric platforms. New technologies and snazzy data visualizations can bring out the worst in designers, tempting us to prioritize a cool factor over solving real problems. It is our duty to remain vigilant so that our work remains grounded in the personas and tasks that drive the need for data in the first place.

Screenshot

Extensive research with district and school leaders helped us identify and prioritize the most impactful questions they need answered in order to do their jobs. Given a somewhat limited scope, we designed a framework that would allow us to get the most bang for our buck up front, using a scalable pattern that can be easily extended as more data is requested.

Screenshot

The landing page of Star CBM Data Insights is organized around the highest priority questions, and provides a high-level answer in the form of a single metric, a trend indicator, and a link to drill in for details wherever there is cause for concern or more investigation.

Screenshot

From the high-level insights provided on the main page, the user can dive in and flexibly analyze data with comparisons over selected timeframes and across schools, grades, teachers, and classes. Since admins have a variety of responsibilities that require getting the data out of our system and into another, we always provide CSV and PDF downloads.

Screenshot

The information contained in these visualizations is useless unless it is actionable. In most cases, that means accessing a sortable list. In this experience, any time the system displays a count of students it also provides a link to see who those students are.
